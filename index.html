My Pockets are Full of Jelly, I Put My Hands in, They Become Sticky but I Keep on Using My Phone

Gelatine, sage, 16 digital pictures (8 minute loop)
2025

I do not delete accidental pocket pictures my phone takes. I find them fascinating in a way that they belong to a certain context, time and place, but they do not represent it almost in any way. Jelly, however, has the capacity to change its form or content based on its context and can represent its surroundings through shape(lessness). To merge these two concepts I converted the phone pictures into 3d meshes, printed them with 3d printer, then made a silicone cast for the 3d shapes. The sage jelly mixture took the place of a picture-turned-3d-model, resulting in the play with the concept of food, post-digitality, and the physical representation of mental processes.

Chocolate and Saliva Covered Sugar Roses

Dried dough objects, video projection (16’03")
2024

Using objects from childhood and archival material, I explore the mental transformation and physical architectural spaces of the past through the materiality of that period. Memories also become a site of action where the past and present meet; through the linking of times, they expand and change form. Different versions of memories then and now persist without the possibility of playback.

Under the Bell Jar

Dried dough objects, video (5’ 32")
2023

Visual, material and textual exploration of the state of derealisation.
I employed the recurring state of derealisation experienced in everyday life as a model/method for perceiving/analyzing the environment. Without questioning the origin of this state, I investigated its manifestations in personal and shared history. Attempting to replicate the state and apply its qualities to various mediums I explore newly emerging connections, creating a new state/space that is both imaginary and physical.

The Impossibility of Seeing the Face of a Person Walking Ahead in the Window Reflections

Vol. 1 - Kalnų parkas (Vilnius, Lithuania) (2023)
Vol. 2 - Rasų kolonija (Vilnius, Lithuania) (2023)
Vol. 3 - Trondheim (Norway) (2025)

Mixed media
2023 - ongoing

The Impossibility of [...] Reflections is a series of walking routes consisting of maps, quizzes and temporary sculptural interventions in the landscape. Two locations in Vilnius have been mapped out so far, each map containing 21 locations with code names. The map for the routes is developed by request and therefore can be created for multiple locations. The walking route is usually individual for the viewer/participant and is determined by the answers obtained from the quiz. The quiz, inspired by the artist’s long-term obsession with BuzzFeed quizzes determining your personality from the selection of foods, offers a dive into each viewer/participant‘s psyche and extracts the most suitable walking path from limited amount of preselected locations. Sculptural interventions in the landscape called ‘altars’ are pop-up structures, which together with a personal walking route offers additional perspective on the ever-changing landscape, both familiar and not, questioning whether subjective experience is still possible.
