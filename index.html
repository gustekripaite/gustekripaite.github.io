Under the Bell Jar

Dried dough objects, video (5’ 32")
2023

Visual, material and textual exploration of the state of derealisation.
I employed the recurring state of derealisation experienced in everyday life as a model/method for perceiving/analyzing the environment. Without questioning the origin of this state, I investigated its manifestations in personal and shared history. Attempting to replicate the state and apply its qualities to various mediums I explore newly emerging connections, creating a new state/space that is both imaginary and physical.

The Impossibility of Seeing the Face of a Person Walking Ahead in the Window Reflections

Vol. 1 - Kalnų parkas (Vilnius, Lithuania)
Vol. 2 - Rasų kolonija (Vilnius, Lithuania)
Vol. 3 - Trondheim (Norway)

Mixed media
2023 - ongoing

The Impossibility of [...] Reflections is a series of walking routes consisting of maps, quizzes and temporary sculptural interventions in the landscape. Two locations in Vilnius have been mapped out so far, each map containing 21 locations with code names. The map for the routes is developed by request and therefore can be created for multiple locations. The walking route is usually individual for the viewer/participant and is determined by the answers obtained from the quiz. The quiz, inspired by the artist’s long-term obsession with BuzzFeed quizzes determining your personality from the selection of foods, offers a dive into each viewer/participant‘s psyche and extracts the most suitable walking path from limited amount of preselected locations. Sculptural interventions in the landscape called ‘altars’ are pop-up structures, which together with a personal walking route offers additional perspective on the ever-changing landscape, both familiar and not, questioning whether subjective experience is still possible. Employing the tools of the time of hyperconnectivity for the hiking opportunity explores the contemporary shifts in the tensions between the subjective and the objective as well as the collective and the individual.

Gustė Kripaitė (b. 2000) lives and creates in Vilnius, Lithuania. She explores thought patterns inspired by the self-help culture and the intentional fictionalisation as a trauma response using personal experiences and more universally recognisable structures from the obsessions of her past. Balancing between nostalgia and curiousness relating to the structural shifts that happened during the period of her childhood she rethinks and employs both her personal and family‘s archive creating links between memory and digital structures.
